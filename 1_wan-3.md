# WAN — Part 3

---

## **1. Evolution to Modern WANs**

### **Q1. Why did modern WAN technologies emerge?**

Because organizations needed:

- Higher speed
- Greater flexibility
- Lower cost
- Better scalability

---

### **Q2. Why are traditional WAN technologies declining?**

Because they are:

- Expensive (leased lines)
- Limited in bandwidth
- Less flexible than modern solutions

---

### **Q3. What is a key characteristic of modern WANs?**

They offer multiple connectivity options based on:

- Cost
- Performance
- Availability

---

## **2. Modern WAN Connectivity Options**

### **Q4. Main categories of modern WAN connectivity**

- Dedicated broadband
- Packet-switched networks
- Internet-based broadband

---

### **Q5. What is dedicated broadband?**

A private high-speed connection where:

- Fiber is directly installed between sites
- Or dark fiber is leased

---

### **Q6. What is dark fiber?**

Unused fiber infrastructure that:

- Can be leased or purchased
- Gives full control to the organization

---

### **Q7. What are modern packet-switched WAN technologies?**

- Metro Ethernet
- MPLS

---

### **Q8. Why is the internet used as a WAN?**

Because it:

- Is widely available
- Is cost-effective
- Supports VPN-based secure communication

---

## **3. Ethernet WAN**

### **Q9. What is Ethernet WAN?**

A WAN service using Ethernet over fiber infrastructure.

---

### **Q10. Alternative names for Ethernet WAN**

- Metro Ethernet (Metro E)
- Ethernet over MPLS (EoMPLS)
- VPLS (Virtual Private LAN Service)

---

### **Q11. Advantages of Ethernet WAN**

- Lower cost
- Easier management
- Simple LAN integration
- Improved productivity

---

### **Q12. Why is Ethernet WAN replacing older technologies?**

Because it is:

- Faster
- Simpler
- More scalable  
  than Frame Relay, ATM, and serial links

---

## **4. MPLS (Advanced Understanding)**

### **Q13. What is MPLS?**

Multiprotocol Label Switching:

- Uses labels instead of IP routing
- Improves forwarding speed and efficiency

---

### **Q14. MPLS vs traditional routing**

- Traditional routing → uses IP lookup at every hop
- MPLS → uses pre-assigned labels for fast forwarding

---

### **Q15. What traffic can MPLS carry?**

- IPv4
- IPv6
- Voice
- Video
- Data

---

### **Q16. What are MPLS routers called?**

Label Switched Routers (LSRs)

---

### **Q17. Types of MPLS routers**

- CE (Customer Edge)
- PE (Provider Edge)
- P (Provider core routers)

---

### **Q18. MPLS advantages**

- QoS (Quality of Service)
- Traffic engineering
- Redundancy
- VPN support

---

## **8. DSL Technology**

### **Q26. What is DSL?**

A broadband technology that:

- Uses telephone lines
- Provides always-on internet

---

### **Q27. Types of DSL**

- ADSL (Asymmetric DSL)
- SDSL (Symmetric DSL)

---

### **Q28. ADSL vs SDSL**

- ADSL → faster download than upload
- SDSL → equal upload and download

---

### **Q29. What affects DSL performance?**

- Distance from Central Office
- Cable quality
- Line condition

---

## **9. DSL Architecture**

### **Q30. What is a DSL modem?**

Device that converts digital signals into DSL signals and vice versa.

---

### **Q31. What is a DSLAM?**

DSL Access Multiplexer:

- Located at provider central office
- Aggregates multiple DSL connections

---

### **Q32. Is DSL a shared medium?**

No. Each user has a dedicated line to the DSLAM.

---

## **10. PPP and DSL**

### **Q33. Why is PPP used in DSL?**

To:

- Authenticate users
- Assign IP addresses
- Monitor connection quality

---

### **Q34. What is PPPoE?**

PPP over Ethernet:

- Encapsulates PPP inside Ethernet frames

---

### **Q35. PPPoE deployment types**

- Host-based (PC client)
- Router-based (router handles PPPoE)

---

## **11. Cable Technology**

### **Q36. What is cable internet?**

Broadband using coaxial cable.

---

### **Q37. What is DOCSIS?**

Standard for high-speed data over cable networks.

---

### **Q38. What is a CMTS?**

Cable Modem Termination System:

- Located at provider headend
- Communicates with cable modems

---

### **Q39. Is cable bandwidth shared?**

Yes:

- Users share bandwidth
- Performance decreases during congestion

---

## **12. Fiber (FTTx Technologies)**

### **Q40. What is FTTx?**

Fiber-to-the-x:

- Fiber deployed close to or at user location

---

### **Q41. Types of FTTx**

- FTTH (Home)
- FTTB (Building)
- FTTN (Neighborhood)

---

### **Q42. Best broadband performance**

Fiber (FTTx):

- Highest bandwidth
- Lowest latency

---

## **13. Wireless WAN Technologies**

### **Q43. What is cellular WAN?**

Internet via mobile networks:

- 3G / 4G / 5G / LTE

---

### **Q44. What is satellite internet?**

Internet using satellites in orbit.

---

### **Q45. What affects satellite performance?**

- Weather
- Physical obstacles
- High latency

---

### **Q46. What is WiMAX?**

Wireless broadband standard for large-area coverage.

---

## **14. VPN Technology**

### **Q47. What is a VPN?**

Encrypted connection over a public network.

---

### **Q48. Why are VPNs important?**

- Data protection
- Secure remote access
- Replace expensive WAN links

---

### **Q49. VPN benefits**

- Cost savings
- Security (encryption)
- Scalability
- Works over broadband

---

### **Q50. Types of VPN**

- Site-to-site VPN
- Remote access VPN

---

### **Q51. What is Site-to-Site VPN?**

Connects entire networks transparently.

---

### **Q52. What is Remote Access VPN?**

User manually connects to network remotely.

---

## **15. ISP Connectivity Models**

### **Q53. What is Single-homed connectivity?**

- One ISP connection
- No redundancy
- Cheapest option

---

### **Q54. What is Dual-homed connectivity?**

- Two links to same ISP
- Redundancy and load balancing

---

### **Q55. What is Multihomed connectivity?**

- Multiple ISPs
- Higher redundancy

---

### **Q56. What is Dual-multihomed?**

- Multiple links to multiple ISPs
- Maximum redundancy
- Most expensive

---

## **16. Broadband Comparison**

### **Q57. Main issue with cable internet**

Shared bandwidth causes congestion.

---

### **Q58. Main limitation of DSL**

- Distance-sensitive
- Lower upload speed

---

### **Q59. Drawback of fiber**

High deployment cost.

---

### **Q60. Issue with cellular internet**

- Coverage limits
- Limited bandwidth

---

### **Q61. Drawback of satellite internet**

- High latency
- Weather sensitivity
- High cost

---

## **17. Key Insight**

### **Q62. Modern vs traditional WANs**

Modern WANs:

- Use shared infrastructure (internet)
- Are flexible and scalable
- Depend on VPNs + broadband

Traditional WANs:

- Use dedicated circuits
- Are expensive and rigid
